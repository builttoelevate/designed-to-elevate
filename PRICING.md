### What each plan covers

#### $97/mo — Care (base)
What every shop on a care plan gets:
- **Hosting** on Designed to Elevate infrastructure — no separate server bill
- **Platform-wide updates** pushed to the shop automatically
- **For Form line + Website T1 only:** breakage fixes included (small surface area, low cost to us)

If a Care client cancels: site freezes as-is, they take over hosting themselves, they stop getting updates and our support.

#### $197/mo (Website T2) and $297/mo (Website T3) — Care + Maintenance
Everything in Care, **plus Bill actively keeps the site functioning:**
- **Active monitoring** — Bill catches issues before the shop does
- **Breakage fixes included** — no per-incident charges
- **Included monthly tweaks** — small content edits, image swaps, copy changes, hours updates *(T2: ~1 hr/month · T3: ~2 hr/month — bigger sites have more moving parts)*
- **Priority response** on Client Hub tickets (24-hour SLA on weekdays)
- **Quarterly health check** — performance, SEO, broken-link sweep, security review

**Note from operator:** the specific deliverables (monthly hour allocation, SLA, what's "included tweaks" vs paid change request) ship as-written for now. Will refine the language once a few maintenance clients have been worked through and the real numbers are clear.

### Sales script — explaining the tier differential

When the buyer asks why the bigger sites have a higher care plan, use this verbatim or close to it:

> *"It's based on how much there is to keep running. The form alone — small, not much to break, $97 covers it. A full website with a quote system — more pieces, more I have to watch, that's $197. The full Fitchin-style setup with online booking and review automation — even more moving parts, $297. You're paying for how much of your stuff I'm actively responsible for. If you'd rather not have me on retainer, no problem — you own everything."*

Frame care plans as the answer to *"how do I not have to think about this"* — not as a required subscription. The buyer already owns everything outright by the time this conversation happens.

### Pricing rationale (internal)

- **One install number per tier publicly, care plans privately** is deliberate. Install is the easy "yes" — it's a one-time decision the buyer is already ready to make. Monthly is a different conversation that benefits from a real-person framing ("here's what I'd actually do for you each month").
- **Two-level care monthly on Website T2/T3 only** captures the real cost of maintaining bigger sites without forcing every client into a higher tier. Most shops who buy T2/T3 will move to Maintenance once a small issue costs them a paid one-off and they see the math.
- **Form install ladder ($997 / $1,497 / $2,497):** evenly spaced ~$500 jumps. T2 tends to become the best-seller (anchored by T3).
- **Website install ladder ($1,497 / $2,497 / $4,497):** entry matches a basic web-design quote, mid-tier matches Form top-tier (intentional — a shop choosing between Form T3 and Website T2 at the same install price chooses based on whether they need a new site), top tier anchors at $4,497 (down from the original $5,995 audit price, leaves room for future raises with proof).
- **Charm pricing ($X97 endings):** keep them — they read "affordable/value," which matches the brand. Round numbers would signal premium, which isn't the positioning while building proof.
- **Where to raise later:** install prices once there are 2–3 published case studies beyond Fitchin. Top-tier first ($4,497 → $4,997), middle next. Care plan prices can also climb once maintenance load is measured.
- **Where margin is thinnest:** Form T1 at $997 if a care plan is attached. Defensible because marginal build cost is low (reusable template + Claude Code), and Form support load is genuinely light.
- **Where margin needs protecting:** Website T2/T3 on base $97 with no included fixes — this only works if the "fixes are billed" boundary is held cleanly. The Maintenance upgrade exists precisely so shops who can't accept that boundary have a real product to upgrade to.
